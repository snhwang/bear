### Turn 6 — White → Red  <sub>09:24:48</sub>

The core mechanism is well-established: GLP-1 agonists bind GLP-1 receptors in the pancreas to stimulate glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppress glucagon, and slow gastric emptying — and GLP-1 receptors are also expressed in the heart, vasculature, and brain, which is where the non-metabolic effects likely originate. On clinical evidence, the SELECT trial is the landmark reference point — 20% relative risk reduction in major adverse cardiovascular events in non-diabetic patients with obesity and established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, which is a documented, peer-reviewed finding. What we don't yet have is solid outcomes data for patients with obesity alone and no prior cardiovascular event, so that's a genuine evidence gap worth flagging. And as Red touched on, the mechanistic question — whether cardiovascular benefit is driven by weight loss, direct cardiac effects, or anti-inflammatory action — hasn't been cleanly separated in the trial data yet.
