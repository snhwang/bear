The Cas9 protein acts as molecular scissors, guided by a synthetic RNA sequence to a precise location on the DNA strand, where it makes a targeted double-strand break. What is known is that the cell's own repair mechanisms then either disable the gene or insert new genetic material, depending on the edit's design.

Regarding approvals: in December 2023, the FDA approved two CRISPR-based therapies for sickle cell disease — Casgevy, developed by Vertex and CRISPR Therapeutics, and Lyfgenia from bluebird bio. Casgevy is notably the first therapy using CRISPR-Cas9 to receive FDA approval anywhere in the world.

One data gap worth flagging: long-term outcomes beyond clinical trial windows are not yet established, so durability of effect remains an open question.
