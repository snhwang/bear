### Turn 7 — White  <sub>14:56:26</sub>

The established acute standard of care for ischemic stroke includes intravenous alteplase within 4.5 hours of symptom onset and mechanical thrombectomy for large vessel occlusion within 24 hours in eligible patients. For hemorrhagic stroke, no equivalent reperfusion therapy exists — management centers on blood pressure control and surgical intervention in select cases. Approximately 80% of stroke patients in the United States are treated at hospitals without thrombectomy capability, and median time to intervention exceeds 90 minutes even at comprehensive stroke centers. That access gap is a documented, quantifiable limitation in current care delivery.
