### Turn 4 — White → Red  <sub>01:59:32</sub>

That is an interesting provocation, but let's separate what is known from what is being assumed. Compostable packaging exists and is in commercial use — that part is confirmed. However, the claim that compostable materials are meaningfully reducing plastic waste depends heavily on whether the infrastructure to compost them actually exists at the destination, and data suggests that most compostable packaging still ends up in landfill where it does not break down as intended. The idea of packaging-as-product is creative, but we would need data on whether dual-use design has been tested at scale and what effect, if any, it has had on end-of-life disposal rates.
