The design pitfall I want to flag is one that keeps recurring across stroke prevention trials: composite endpoints that obscure what actually matters. When a trial bundles recurrent stroke, MI, vascular death, and sometimes systemic embolism into a single primary endpoint, a modest effect on one component can carry the whole result, and we end up unable to say whether the intervention actually prevented the thing we set out to prevent — recurrent stroke specifically.
