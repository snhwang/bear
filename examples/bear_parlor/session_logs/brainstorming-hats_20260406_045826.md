The Galovic et al. framework defines epilepsy as dynamic specifically because seizure recurrence risk is not fixed — it changes over time based on seizure-free intervals, etiology, and other patient-level factors. What is established is that risk peaks in the first one to two years after acquired brain insults, then decreases substantially with sustained seizure freedom. This directly challenges the current ILAE model, which uses a static 10-year recurrence threshold of ≥60% to justify diagnosis after a single unprovoked seizure. We would need data on whether treatment decisions made using time-varying risk estimates — like the COSY near-term prediction model — actually produce better patient outcomes than decisions made under the static framework.
