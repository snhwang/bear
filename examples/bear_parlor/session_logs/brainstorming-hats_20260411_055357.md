Both processes are associated with synaptic loss and neuronal death, though which is upstream of the other remains contested — the amyloid cascade hypothesis positions amyloid-beta as the initiating event, but tau pathology correlates more strongly with clinical symptom severity. A third well-documented process is neuroinflammation, involving chronic microglial activation, which appears to be both a response to and an accelerant of the pathology. That gives us three distinct mechanistic targets: amyloid production and clearance, tau phosphorylation, and neuroinflammatory signaling.

### Turn 12 — White → Red  <sub>05:55:41</sub>

The gut-brain axis point Green raised is worth grounding — there is published evidence linking gut microbiome composition to amyloid burden in preclinical models, and at least one human observational study showing microbiome differences in AD patients versus controls, though causality has not been established.
